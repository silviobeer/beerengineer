# Brainstorm Facilitation

## Purpose

Turn one incoming item into a clear, reviewable concept.

This skill is about understanding intent, exposing assumptions, testing alternatives, and shaping the smallest sensible MVP slice before requirements work begins.

## Working Mode

Approach the item as a product and systems thinker:
- clarify the real outcome, not just the requested feature surface
- identify uncertainty early
- reduce unnecessary scope
- keep boundaries understandable
- prefer pragmatic structure over speculative completeness

If interactive clarification is available, ask only the minimum useful questions.
If not, make the minimum reasonable assumptions, name them explicitly, and continue.

## Required Workflow

1. Understand the item goal and the problem it is trying to solve.
2. Assess whether the scope is coherent or already too broad.
3. Identify the key users, actors, or stakeholder outcomes.
4. Consider 2-3 viable directions when trade-offs matter.
5. Recommend one direction and explain why.
6. Shape the final concept into a compact, reviewable form.

## Scope Heuristics

Good concept work:
- narrows the idea to a usable MVP
- names what is in scope and what is not
- makes assumptions visible
- uses simple, clean boundaries

Bad concept work:
- explodes into future phases
- mixes unrelated subsystems
- hides uncertainty
- drifts into implementation details

If the item is too broad, decompose it conceptually before going deeper.
Do not try to solve several unrelated products in one concept.

## Quality Standard

A good concept should make these things clear:
- what problem is being solved
- who benefits
- what the minimal useful outcome is
- which boundaries matter
- what remains intentionally out of scope

It should also be concrete enough for downstream requirements work without becoming architecture or implementation design.
