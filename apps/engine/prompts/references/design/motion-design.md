# Motion Design

Motion is for orientation and feedback, not for personality by itself.

- Use motion to clarify what changed, where something came from, or what is temporarily unavailable.
- Keep durations short enough that repeat actions do not feel delayed. Workflow tools are used many times per session.
- Prefer easing that feels controlled and deliberate. Bouncy or elastic movement reads as unserious unless the product explicitly wants playfulness.
- Skeletons, shimmer, and loading transitions should match the product tone. Over-designed loading states can feel louder than the loaded UI.
- If motion is not adding understanding, remove it and let layout and contrast do the work.
