# Spatial Design

Spacing and container logic should explain the product structure at a glance.

- Use spacing rhythm to show grouping. Related controls should sit closer together than unrelated sections.
- Prefer one strong container boundary over multiple soft nested ones. Structure should feel decisive, not padded into submission.
- Keep max widths honest to the task. Analytical screens can stretch wider than marketing pages, but not until scanning breaks.
- Treat section padding and card padding as separate jobs. Page rhythm and local content density usually need different values.
- Borders, radius, and shadows should reinforce the product stance. Sharp and direct is different from soft and ambient; choose, do not drift.
- Empty space should reveal hierarchy or protect readability. If it only makes the UI look expensive in a screenshot, it is probably waste.
